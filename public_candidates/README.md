# Public Candidates

This directory is a holding layer for concepts and structures with strong potential for future publication.

`public_candidates/` is not where already-published material lives.
It temporarily holds things that seem reproducible enough to be extracted from internal operation and reused, referenced, or forked by others.

## What Belongs Here

- concepts that do not depend too heavily on raw personal logs
- operational frames that can be explained structurally
- management concepts that could connect to tools or public-facing translation
- highly reproducible material that appears portable to others
- fragments or local principles that already have value even without being fully integrated

## What Does Not Belong Here

- raw personal history itself
- material that cannot yet be read without personal context
- material that overexposes vulnerability
- items still clearly inside the hypothesis stage
- layers like `narratives/` that preserve internal cross-cutting reading lines themselves
- pathways that could accelerate self-mythologization or myth-system formation by weaving fragments together
- final-stage functions such as essay generation or core candidacy that automatically bind material into a higher narrative

## Publication Safety Policy

In public versions, it is acceptable to expose fragments, local principles, and operational frames.
What should be limited more carefully is the pathway that compresses those pieces into a coherent higher-order narrative.

The danger lies less in the fragments themselves than in the moment when multiple fragments and operational heuristics begin to read as a unified mythic system.
For that reason, a public starter kit should normally avoid including automatic promotion toward essay generation, core candidacy, or cross-cutting integration.
`narratives/` is an internal layer specifically for handling that integrative force, so it should usually stay out of public releases.

## Flow

1. Something becomes structured internally
2. If needed, it becomes subject to layer management or directory management
3. If it seems publication-ready, move it into `public_candidates/`
4. If it needs translation for external readers, send it to `interfaces/public_translation/`
5. If it should be cut out as a practical tool, send it to `interfaces/cognitive_tools/`
